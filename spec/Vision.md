# The APP Vision

## Building the Trust Layer for Autonomous Systems

The internet was built for humans.

Its foundational protocols assume that people, organizations, and applications are the primary participants.

DNS tells us where systems are located.

SSL certificates tell us who operates them.

OAuth determines who may access them.

Audit systems record what happened.

For decades, this model has worked.

The rise of autonomous systems changes everything.

---

## The Emergence of the Agent Internet

Software is no longer limited to responding to requests.

Agents are beginning to:

* Search
* Reason
* Negotiate
* Purchase
* Coordinate
* Delegate
* Act autonomously

Soon, billions of autonomous systems will interact with one another across organizations, platforms, and jurisdictions.

The world is rapidly moving from:

```text
Human → Software
```

to:

```text
Human → Agent → Agent → Agent
```

The infrastructure supporting this transition does not yet exist.

---

## The Missing Layer

Today's autonomous systems lack fundamental trust infrastructure.

There is no universal mechanism to determine:

* Who owns an agent
* Whether an agent is authentic
* What actions an agent performed
* Whether an agent has been revoked
* Which organization issued it
* What authority it possesses
* Whether it can be trusted

The traditional internet solved these challenges for websites and applications.

The agent internet has not.

---

## Trust Must Be Portable

Trust cannot be locked inside individual platforms.

A future where every vendor maintains its own isolated identity system creates fragmentation, lock-in, and reduced interoperability.

Agent identity should be portable.

Ownership should be verifiable.

Trust should be transferable.

Provenance should be inspectable.

Reputation should be explainable.

Authorization should be auditable.

---

## Open Infrastructure Matters

The infrastructure of autonomous systems should not depend on a single company.

History has repeatedly demonstrated the value of open protocols.

The internet succeeded because its foundations remained open.

Email is open.

DNS is open.

HTTP is open.

TLS is open.

The infrastructure of autonomous systems should be open as well.

APP exists to help establish those foundations.

---

## Federation Over Centralization

No single organization should control the identity and trust infrastructure of autonomous systems.

APP adopts a federated model.

Organizations may operate their own nodes.

Trust relationships may be established across domains.

Verification may occur without centralized control.

The protocol is designed to encourage cooperation while preserving independence.

---

## Verifiable Agency

As agents become more capable, society will increasingly require accountability.

Questions such as:

* Who authorized this action?
* Which agent performed it?
* Can the claim be verified?
* Can the action be audited?

must have clear answers.

APP provides a framework for establishing those answers.

---

## Our Mission

To create open standards for identity, ownership, provenance, authorization, reputation, delegation, and federation in the emerging agent internet.

To ensure trust remains portable.

To ensure ownership remains verifiable.

To ensure autonomous systems remain accountable.

To help build the trust layer for autonomous systems.

---

## The Future

Just as DNS became the naming layer of the internet.

Just as SSL became the trust layer of the web.

APP aspires to become a foundational trust layer for autonomous systems.

The work has only begun.

We invite developers, researchers, enterprises, governments, and protocol builders to participate in shaping the future of trusted autonomy.
